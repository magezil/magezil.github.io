Crochet for Charity

I have started looking into donating items that I crochet to charity. The charities that I am most interested in are Project Linus, Crochet for Cancer and Bay Area Cancer Connections. Both of these non-profit organizations accept handmade items, including those made by crochet, to provide comfort to those in need and accept donations near where I live.

Project Linus

Project Linus provides blankets to children in need. I like this organization because they support children who are seriously ill, traumatized, or otherwise in need. Just like Linus from Peanuts who always had his blanket with him, I know how comforting a blanket can be. When I was growing up, I had a pillow that I took with me everywhere. I think it will be more meaningful to give a child something that helps them feel safe when they are already in a situation where they are vulnerable due to illness or trauma rather than sell the blanket instead.

Crochet for Cancer and Bay Area Cancer Connections

Both Crochet for Cancer and Bay Area Cancer Connections provide hats to cancer patients who are undergoing chemotherapy or other treatments. A number of patients may feel insecure because they are losing their hair so receiving the hat helps them feel more sure about themselves and keep their head warm. I also know many people who have had or currently have cancer, including people in my family.
